Director

 
 

Stan Wells began directing and teaching comedy improvisation twenty-five years ago in Los Angeles. He is a graduate of the University of Washington’s Professional Actors training program and appeared in numerous television and film productions before discovering improvisational comedy.  He directed The Groundlings in the late 1980′s working with such TV notables as Kathy Griffin and Julia Sweeney.

He helped to create and subsequently directed, The Trans4merz more than twenty years ago, a group that is still performing in the LA area. Members of The Trans4merz included, Lisa Kudrow (Friends, The Comeback), Cynthia Stevenson , Emmy winning SNL writer, Scott Wainio, Emmy winning writers, Jay Kogen (Frasier, The Simpsons, Tracy Ullman Show) John Stark, (Cheers, Ellen, According to Jim), John Frink (The Simpsons) along with writer/performers Harry Hannigan (According to Jim), Ted Michaels, founder of The Crazy Uncle Joe Show at The Groundlings theater, Mike Castagnola who appeared in numerous film roles and counts former Groundlings Sunday Company pal Conan O’Brien as a good friend. Conan performed with The Trans4merz on several occasions as did comedian/actors Jon Lovitz and Rik Overton.

Members of the hit online shows The Guild and Legend of Neil, Felicia Day, Sandeep Parikh, Jeff Lewis, Alex Albrecht and Mike Rose met taking classes at The Empty Stage and went on to  perform with, Being Humans, POP! and The Waterbrains. They are continuing to perform live improv in the LA area and around the country as, Hammer Don’t Hurt ‘Em, a show based on the clap-in technique taught to them by director, Stan Wells.

Actress/comedienne Kristin Wiig (SNL) performed for several years at The Empty Stage with The Waterbrains and The Perks.

The Empty Stage closed its doors in 2008 and Stan moved back to his hometown to teach a whole new group of students. In the last three years he has taught improv for the third year actors at the University of Washington’s PATP program, founded a NW version of Being Humans and recently closed a successful six month run of The Fortune Room, an improvised lounge show at The Little Red Bistro in Seattle.
